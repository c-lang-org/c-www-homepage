C was designed by Dennis Ritchie during the early 1970s at Bell Labs as an improvement upon the B language by Ken Thompson. After the release of the Unix operating system, and later also publication of the first edition of the bestseller book The C Programming Language, authored by Brian Kernighan together with Ritchie (K&R), C became one of the most widely used programming languages in the world, with implementations for practically all architectures and systems.

You can learn more from the materials listed in the resources!

Standardization

After the book, the first official standardization effort was led by X3J1I Technical Committee under project 381-D by ANSI on Computers and Information Processing (X3).

The current copyright holder of the C standard is ISO and the work on the project is overseen by working group ISO/IEC JTC1/SC22/WG14.

The principles guiding the committee in its efforts are outlined in the document The C Standard charter.
